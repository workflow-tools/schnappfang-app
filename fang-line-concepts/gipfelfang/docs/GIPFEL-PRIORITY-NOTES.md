# Gipfel Priority Notes

## Overview

Gipfelfang prioritizes 16 summits across Bayern and the broader Alps using four scoring criteria. This document tracks decision rationale for tier assignment and guides content strategy.

**Tier 1:** Iconic summits everyone knows — highest visit likelihood + story richness
**Tier 2:** Distinctive characters with strong stories — geological or cultural hooks
**Tier 3:** Solid hiker favourites — reliable accessibility + photo appeal
**Tier 4:** Unique angles — each brings a different user motivation (geology, history, sport)

---

## Scoring Criteria

### Criterion A: Visit Likelihood
**Accessibility, trail infrastructure, popularity baseline**

- **Score 5:** Seilbahn access + trail-marked routes + >10,000 visitors/year
- **Score 4:** Multiple route options + good infrastructure + 5,000–10,000 visitors/year
- **Score 3:** Accessible but less frequented + moderate infrastructure
- **Score 2:** Specialized access (via ferrata, scramble) or remote location
- **Score 1:** Expert-only, rare visits

**Why this matters:** Gipfelfang cards are collectible; higher visit likelihood = more captures in the wild.

---

### Criterion B: Story Richness
**History, geology, cultural significance, human narrative**

- **Score 5:** Multiple story layers (cultural + geological + historical + ecological)
- **Score 4:** Three strong story dimensions; widely known in Alpine culture
- **Score 3:** One or two story dimensions; local or regional significance
- **Score 2:** Limited narrative angle; mostly geographic importance
- **Score 1:** Primarily technical interest; minimal cultural/historical narrative

**Why this matters:** Strong stories drive social sharing, content creation, and emotional attachment.

---

### Criterion C: Bayern Geography
**Location within Bayerische Alpen, Bayerischer Wald, or DACH-adjacent classic routes**

- **Score 5:** Core Bayerische Alpen (Zugspitze, Watzmann region) or historical Ludwig-II. routes
- **Score 4:** Major Bavarian Alpine sub-ranges (Allgäu, Berchtesgaden, Chiemgau)
- **Score 3:** Bavarian Wald or accessible from Bayern (Vorarlberg border)
- **Score 2:** Northern Alps accessible from Bayern but primarily Austrian/Swiss
- **Score 1:** Peripheral; requires crossing multiple borders

**Why this matters:** Gipfelfang is a Bayern-centric app; geography drives discoverability via map, weather, and local community.

---

### Criterion D: Photo Appeal
**Panorama distinctiveness, summit cross presence, dramatic terrain, seasonal variation**

- **Score 5:** Iconic summit cross + dramatic panorama + seasonal drama (Föhn, sunrise, snow)
- **Score 4:** Strong visual signature + memorable terrain + good lighting conditions
- **Score 3:** Attractive views + reasonable composition angles
- **Score 2:** Scenic but visually generic or difficult lighting
- **Score 1:** Limited photogenic value or featureless

**Why this matters:** Gipfelfang UI is image-centric; strong visual identity drives app store presence and user engagement.

## Content Strategy Implications

### Tier 1 (4 peaks)
- **Card rarity:** Most common — initial onboarding defaults
- **Guide depth:** Full route options, safety warnings, cultural deep-dives
- **Imagery:** Multiple seasonal variations, sunrise/sunset variants, drone panoramas
- **Social amplification:** Feature on app launch, marketing materials, collaborations

### Tier 2 (4 peaks)
- **Card rarity:** Very common — core collection
- **Guide depth:** 1–2 primary routes + specialist route (via ferrata or winter)
- **Imagery:** Seasonal + unique geological/historical elements
- **Social amplification:** Secondary promotion, hiking community highlights

### Tier 3 (4 peaks)
- **Card rarity:** Common — everyday trails
- **Guide depth:** Straightforward 1 route + day-hike context
- **Imagery:** Standard panorama + seasonal variation
- **Social amplification:** Community-driven, user submissions prioritized

### Tier 4 (4 peaks)
- **Card rarity:** Uncommon — specialized interest
- **Guide depth:** Specialist context (history, sports, geology focus)
- **Imagery:** Targeted to unique angle (architecture, ladders, artifacts)
- **Social amplification:** Niche communities (via ferrata clubs, Ludwig enthusiasts, paragliders)
