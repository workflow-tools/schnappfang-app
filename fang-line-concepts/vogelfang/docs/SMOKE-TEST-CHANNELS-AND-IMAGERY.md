# Vogelfang: Smoke Test Channels & Imagery Strategy

**Stand: April 2026**

---

## Part 1: Channel Strategy

### Overview

Vogelfang targets birdwatchers in the DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland) across social platforms and communities where birders congregate. Mirrors Pilzfang's multi-channel approach but adapted for birding communities' communication patterns.

---

### Channel Priority Matrix

| Channel | Primary Audience | Content Type | Best Practices | Priority |
|---------|------------------|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Pinterest** | Female 35-65, garden interest, visual inspiration | Hi-res bird photography, info-graphics on species identification, seasonal guides | Long pins (max 1000px tall), keyword optimization for "Vogelidentifikation", "Gartenvögel", boards by region | High |
| **Instagram** | Younger birders 25-45, photo-sharing habit, aesthetic-focused | Carousel posts (species comparisons), Reels (bird sounds/behavior), Stories (daily sightings), IG shop linked to app | Hashtags: #VogelIdentifikation #DACHVögel #Gartenvögel #Vogelbeobachtung, location tags (regional), video-first Reels | High |
| **NABU Forums** | Dedicated ornithologists, regional chapters, expert network | Engagement as brand partner, species spotting announcements, conservation updates, expert QA threads | Thread participation, not self-promotion, coordinate with regional NABU Gruppen | Medium |
| **eBird Germany/Austria/Switzerland** | Data-driven birders, Citizen Science, species documentation | eBird Hotspots integration, "rare sighting" alerts, checklist syncing (future), species distributions | Community checklist features, regional hotspot maps, observation coordination | Medium |
| **Facebook Birding Groups** | Regional birders 40-70, community-focused, WhatsApp-adjacent | Species sighting alerts, regional group partnerships, monthly bird challenges, elder-friendly content | Local group targeting (state/regional level), partnerships with Vogelschutzgruppen, simple UI explanations | Medium |
| **TikTok** | Young birders 18-35, audio-visual storytelling, trend-following | 15-60s bird behavior clips (rütteln, drumming, diving), species challenges, migration route animations | Sound-first (bird calls as hooks), trending sounds, duets with birding creators, #birdtok | Low (experimental) |
| **Twitter/X** | Ornithologists, birding journalists, conservation orgs | Breaking research, migration phenology data, species spotting coordination, retweet campaigns | Data threads, thread storms on seasonal patterns, minimal self-promotion | Low (informational) |
