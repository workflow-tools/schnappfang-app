# Vogelfang v0.1 Species Priority Framework

**Stand: April 2026**

---

## Overview

Vogelfang (Birdwatching for DACH) launches with a curated 16-species smoke test dataset, modeled on Schnappfang's four-tier prioritization system. Species selection prioritizes photograph-likelihood, fun-fact richness, Central European geography, and visual distinctiveness.

---

## Selection Criteria

### Criterion A: Photo-Likelihood (Garden & Park Birds First)

**Rationale:** User success depends on visible, accessible subjects. Common garden and park birds maximize early user engagement and build confidence.

- **Tier 1 priority:** Highly visible in suburban gardens (Rotkehlchen, Amsel, Blaumeise, Buntspecht)
- **Tier 2 priority:** Visible in urban parks and larger gardens (Eisvogel, Stieglitz, Kleiber, Zaunkönig)
- **Tier 3 priority:** Require dedicated fieldwork but distinctive (Mäusebussard, Turmfalke, Graureiher, Kormoran)
- **Tier 4 priority:** Niche observation or migration-dependent (Mauersegler, Weißstorch, Sperber, Schwarzspecht)

**Evidence:**
- NABU garden bird survey data shows Rotkehlchen and Amsel top-5 observations
- iNaturalist DACH region confirms Blaumeise and Buntspecht as most-photographed winter birds
- Eisvogel and Stieglitz require water/wildflower habitat but highly photogenic when accessible

---

### Criterion B: Fun-Info Richness

**Rationale:** SnapCard value proposition depends on curated micro-content: etymology, fun facts, song descriptions, migration patterns. Species with rich cultural/behavioral stories drive engagement.

- **Rotkehlchen:** Christmas symbol (cultural), red breast development (behavioral)
- **Blaumeise:** Milk bottle intelligence (behavioral), winter flocking (ecological)
- **Eisvogel:** Diving physiology (behavioral), water quality indicator (ecological)
- **Stieglitz:** Distelfink etymology (linguistic), acrobatic seed-eating (behavioral)
- **Zaunkönig:** Disproportionate loudness (behavioral), winter communal roosting (behavioral)
- **Weißstorch:** Ammenmythos (cultural), intercontinental migration (geographic)
- **Schwarzspecht:** Acoustic power (behavioral), rectangular hole signature (identification)

**High-richness species:** Zaunkönig, Stieglitz, Eisvogel, Weißstorch
**Medium-richness species:** Rotkehlchen, Blaumeise, Kleiber, Buntspecht, Mäusebussard
**Lower-richness species:** Amsel (familiar but fewer surprises), Turmfalke (rütteln is main story)

## Tier Justifications

### Tier 1: Ubiquitous Garden Birds (4 species)

**Selection logic:** First-time birders encounter these in home gardens. High confidence + early success.

| Species | Why Tier 1 |
|---------|-----------|
| **Rotkehlchen** | Every German garden. Red breast unmistakable. Cultural icon. Rich etymology. |
| **Amsel** | Most vocal garden bird. Year-round presence. Black male distinctive. |
| **Blaumeise** | Winter feeders frequent. Blue cap unique. Intelligent behavior. |
| **Buntspecht** | Distinctive drumming. Acrobatic and visible. Black-white-red unmistakable. |

**Expected user engagement:** 90%+ of early users will photograph ≥1 Tier 1 species in first week.

---

### Tier 2: Distinctive Specialty Birds (4 species)

**Selection logic:** Beautiful, memorable, require minor habitat specificity but reliably observable. High "wow factor."

| Species | Why Tier 2 |
|---------|-----------|
| **Eisvogel** | Brightest color in temperate Europe. Requires water access but unforgettable. Diving behavior unique. |
| **Stieglitz** | Most colorful passerine. Increasing in gardens and parks. Distelfink cultural history. Acrobatic. |
| **Kleiber** | Unique upside-down behavior. Nutty vocalizations. Present in parks/woodlots. |
| **Zaunkönig** | Disproportionate personality. Loudest songbird. Hidden but vocal. Winter communal roosting unique. |

**Expected user engagement:** 60%+ of engaged users will photograph ≥1 Tier 2 species by week 3.

---

### Tier 3: Large, Dramatic Birds (4 species)

**Selection logic:** Impressive size/presence. Raptors and water birds attract dedicated fieldwork. Ecological/cultural relevance.

| Species | Why Tier 3 |
|---------|-----------|
| **Mäusebussard** | Most common raptor. Rütteln behavior distinctive. Open-country iconic. Variable plumage interesting. |
| **Turmfalke** | Urban success story. Rütteln behavior very distinctive. Accessible on church towers. |
| **Graureiher** | Impressive size. Still-hunting patience dramatic. Colony sites exist. Fischerei-Konflikt cultural relevance. |
| **Kormoran** | Impressive size. Wing-drying posture unique. Recolonization story (Bestands-Erfolg). Conflict/conservation angle. |

**Expected user engagement:** 40%+ of dedicated users will photograph ≥1 Tier 3 species by month 2.

---

### Tier 4: Niche/Migration/Specialist Species (4 species)

**Selection logic:** Specialized habitats, migration windows, or cultural significance justify inclusion despite lower photo-likelihood.

| Species | Why Tier 4 |
|---------|-----------|
| **Mauersegler** | Summer-only, dramatic aerial acrobatics. Urban presence but narrow season (May-Aug). Sleeping-in-flight unique. |
| **Weißstorch** | Restoration success story (national significance). Ammenmythos cultural. Limited range but 100% unmistakable. |
| **Sperber** | Difficult-to-photograph raptor but commonness and dimorphism justify inclusion. Jagd-Konflikt cultural. |
| **Schwarzspecht** | Old-growth forest specialist. Largest specht (45cm impressive). Rectangular holes diagnostic. Alt-Holz conservation angle. |

**Expected user engagement:** 10-20% of specialist users will photograph ≥1 Tier 4 species by month 3.

## Coverage Gaps & v0.2 Candidates

### Identified gaps:

1. **Alpine specialists:** Alpendohle, Schneehuhn, Bergpieper (high-altitude, DACH-specific)
2. **Nocturnal/secretive:** Uhu, Waldkauz, Waldohreule, Raufußkauz (owl specialists missing)
3. **Wading birds:** Teichhuhn, Blässhuhn, Haubentaucher (water diversity narrow)
4. **Song thrushes:** Singdrossel, Rotdrossel (summer migrants, song-rich)
5. **Warblers:** Mönchsgrasmücke, Gartengrasmücke (spring/summer voice-dependent)
6. **Finches:** Kernbeißer, Gimpel (winter finch irruptions, cultural)
7. **Grebes/divers:** Haubentaucher, Zwergtaucher (water specialist behavior)
8. **Crows/jays:** Eichelhäher, Rabenkrähe (common but less "fun-fact rich" than selection)

### v0.2 Priority Candidates (by engagement potential):

**High priority:**
- **Haubentaucher** (Podiceps cristatus) — Summer grebes, distinctive crests, courtship displays, water sports conflict
- **Uhu** (Bubo bubo) — Largest owl, recolonization story, nocturnal challenge, acoustic power
- **Alpendohle** (Pyrrhocorax graculus) — Alpine endemic, DACH geographic expansion
- **Mönchsgrasmücke** (Sylvia atricapilla) — Summer breeder, rich song, increasingly overwintering

**Medium priority:**
- **Teichhuhn** (Gallinula chloropus) — Garden water birds, behavioral drama, chick-rearing
- **Kernbeißer** (Coccothraustes coccothraustes) — Explosive bill adaptation, winter irruptions, nutcracker ecology
- **Waldkauz** (Strix aluco) — Commonest owl, calling surveys, nocturnal access

**Lower priority:**
- **Rabenkrähe** (Corvus corone) — Common but less distinctive than current selection
- **Singdrossel** (Turdus philomelos) — Beautiful song but morphologically subtle
- **Zwergtaucher** (Tachybaptus ruficollis) — Small, water-dependent, low visibility

## Implementation Notes

### Photo sourcing priority:
1. **User-generated (iNaturalist, eBird)** — DACH region, verified observations
2. **Wikimedia Commons** — CC-licensed, species-specific galleries
3. **Macaulay Library** — Cornell Lab, high-quality behaviors and calls
4. **NABU official galleries** — Brand consistency, locally-sourced

### Sound/call sourcing:
1. **Xeno-canto** — Crowdsourced bird recordings, DACH-region emphasis
2. **Macaulay Library** — Behavioral recordings (drumming, rütteln, song)
3. **NABU Vogelstimmen** — Official German reference

### Content localization:
- All species names: German + Latin (dual naming)
- Etymology: German language origin (althochdeutsch, mittelhochdeutsch, or borrowed)
- Fun facts: DACH-relevant (cultural icons, migration patterns, local conservation status)
- Conservation status: IUCN + German Red List (Rote Liste Deutschland/Länder)
- Seasonal hints: DACH observation calendar (migration windows, breeding season)

---

## Success Metrics (v0.1 → v0.2)

- User photo completion rate by tier (target: Tier 1 ≥80%, Tier 2 ≥50%, Tier 3 ≥20%)
- Engagement depth: fun-fact reads, song plays, etymology clicks
- Geographic distribution: Ensure usage across all DACH regions
- Seasonal accuracy: Verify migration hints against iNaturalist observations
- Community feedback: User requests for "missing" species (candidate feature requests)
