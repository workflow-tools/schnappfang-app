# Tauchfang Species Priority Framework

## Overview

Species prioritization for the Tauchfang diving domain follows four criteria adapted from the Schnappfang fishing framework. Each criterion is weighted to ensure the app surfaces species that divers in the DACH region and Mediterranean will encounter, photograph, and find genuinely interesting.

---

## Prioritization Criteria

### Criterion A: Encounter-Likelihood (Common Species in DACH + Mediterranean)

**Weight:** 30%

Measures the probability a recreational diver (PADI Open Water to Divemaster level) will encounter the species in target diving regions.

**Scoring:**
- **5 (Very High):** Species encountered 60%+ of dives in specific region (e.g., Seepferdchen in Posidonia meadows, Zackenbarsch in Mediterranean reefs)
- **4 (High):** Encountered 30-60% of dives seasonally (e.g., Muräne, Rochen in summer)
- **3 (Moderate):** Encountered 10-30% of dives (e.g., Oktopus, Rotfeuerfisch since invasion)
- **2 (Low):** Occasional encounters (e.g., Blauhai pelagic dives)
- **1 (Rare):** Bucket-list encounters only (e.g., Delfin, Schildkröte)

**DACH-specific modifiers:**
- Freshwater species (Flusskrebs, Hecht, Wels) score +1 tier in DACH lakes (Bodensee, Zürichsee, Chiemsee, Bavarian lakes)
- Mediterranean species score -1 tier in north Adriatic (smaller populations)
- Invasive species (Rotfeuerfisch) score +1 tier for novelty interest despite lower ecological encounter frequency

**Source data:** Dive logs from taucher.net, Diveboard regional breakdowns, Mediterranean dive operators' species checklists (Blue Planet Diving, H2O Center Malta)

---

### Criterion B: Fun-Info Richness (Engagement & Memorable Facts)

**Weight:** 25%

Measures the density and memorability of genuine scientific/cultural facts about the species that excite divers and create shareable moments.

**Scoring:**
- **5 (Exceptionally Rich):** Multiple "wow factor" facts: Oktopus (intelligence + colour-change speed + tool use), Mondfisch (heaviest bony fish + bizarre shape), Wels (catfish barbels + size records)
- **4 (Very Rich):** 2-3 standout facts: Muräne (7000 teeth + territorial + long lifespan), Seepferdchen (male pregnancy + tiny size + monogamous), Rotfeuerfisch (invasive + venomous + no predators)
- **3 (Rich):** 1-2 notable facts: Blauhai (open-water wanderer + 50+ km/day), Schildkröte (magnetic navigation + site fidelity)
- **2 (Moderate):** Basic interesting facts: Hecht (ambush hunter + speed), Seestern (stomach eversion)
- **1 (Minimal):** Limited fun facts: generic small reef fish

**Content types that score high:**
- Evolutionary oddities (Mondfisch, Seepferdchen)
- Invasive/ecological stories (Rotfeuerfisch)
- Record-breaking attributes (Wels size, Oktopus longevity)
- Behavioral drama (Hecht ambush, Delfin intelligence, Muräne territorial displays)
- Conservation status (endangered species = narrative tension)
- Symbiosis/ecology (Oktopus camouflage + hunting, Seepferdchen monogamy + brood care)

---

### Criterion C: DACH + Mediterranean Geography (Regional Relevance)

**Weight:** 25%

Measures species accessibility from primary Tauchfang markets: German-speaking divers, Mediterranean visitors, freshwater DACH lake enthusiasts.

**Scoring:**
- **5 (Primary):** Year-round or seasonal abundance in core regions: Mediterranean (Balearics, Croatia, Malta, Greece), DACH Lakes (Bodensee, Zürichsee, Chiemsee)
- **4 (Secondary):** Found in extended Med region or northern DACH waters: northern Adriatic, Atlantic coast (Portuguese dives), larger Alpine lakes
- **3 (Tertiary):** Migratory/seasonal presence: summer pelagic encounters, seasonal freshwater migrations (pike spawning), invasive range expansions
- **2 (Visitor):** Occasional/accidental encounters: northward range shifts (Rotfeuerfisch colonization), single-location species
- **1 (Bucket-list only):** Must travel far or wait for rare conditions: deep pelagic encounters, specialized expeditions

**Geography weighting:**
- Mediterranean Tier 1-2 species: +1 point (core market)
- DACH freshwater Tier 1-3 species: +1 point (domestic market)
- Invasive species with expansion: +0.5 point (emerging interest)
- Pelagic-only (Blauhai, Mondfisch, Delfin): no bonus

---

### Criterion D: Underwater Photo Appeal (Visual Impact)

**Weight:** 20%

Measures the species' photogenic qualities and appeal to the camera-centric dive community.

**Scoring:**
- **5 (Highly Photogenic):** Striking coloration, distinctive shape, interesting behavior, macro or wide-angle appeal: Chromodoris (jewel colours), Oktopus (texture + pose variety), Zackenbarsch (dark + angular), Rotfeuerfisch (symmetrical fins)
- **4 (Very Photogenic):** Good coloration or dramatic silhouette: Muräne (golden in caves), Seepferdchen (unique shape + small scale), Hecht (sleek + predatory), Wels (catfish barbels)
- **3 (Photogenic):** Decent visual appeal, requires composition skill: Seestern (texture), Rochen (buried in sand), Flusskrebs (claws + habitat)
- **2 (Moderately Appealing):** Harder to photograph or less visually striking: Blauhai (open water + fast moving), Mondfisch (unusual but hard to frame)
- **1 (Challenging to Photograph):** Rare, fast, small, or behaviorally difficult: Delfin (fast pelagic), Süßwasserqualle (tiny + transparent), Schildkröte (skittish)

**Visual factors considered:**
- Colour richness (Chromodoris, Rotfeuerfisch, Seepferdchen)
- Textural detail (Oktopus skin, Muräne pattern, Seestern spines)
- Behavioural interest (Oktopus hunting, Hecht ambush from weed, Delfin pod interaction)
- Macro vs. wide-angle versatility (small species like Seepferdchen, large like Zackenbarsch)
- Habitat context appeal (Posidonia meadows for Seepferdchen, cave reefs for Muräne)
- Night dive visibility (Oktopus, Flusskrebs only active nocturnally in photography)

## Species Tier Assignments

### Tier 1: Must-Have (A=4-5, B=4-5, C=4-5, D=4-5)

Divers expect these in the app; they're common in target regions, photo-worthy, and information-rich.

- **Oktopus** (Octopus vulgaris): A=4 (30-60% Mediterranean summer dives), B=5 (intelligence + camouflage + tool use), C=5 (abundant Med), D=5 (stunning macro + behaviour)
- **Muräne** (Muraena helena): A=4 (30-60% Mediterranean reef dives), B=4 (7000 teeth + territorial), C=5 (iconic Med), D=4 (golden in caves, dramatic head shots)
- **Zackenbarsch** (Epinephelus marginatus): A=4 (50%+ deep Med dives if present), B=4 (largest raubfisch + colour flashing), C=5 (flagship Med), D=4 (dark + silhouette)
- **Seepferdchen** (Hippocampus hippocampus): A=5 (70%+ Posidonia meadow dives summer), B=5 (male pregnancy + monogamy + tiny), C=5 (seasonal Mediterranean), D=5 (macro perfection)

---

### Tier 2: Dramatic Encounters (A=2-4, B=4-5, C=3-4, D=3-4)

Exciting "wow" species with good engagement potential; less common but memorable.

- **Rotfeuerfisch** (Pterois miles): A=3 (growing 20-40% Med encounters), B=4 (invasive story + venomous + no predators), C=3 (emerging since 2000s), D=5 (symmetrical fins + intricate)
- **Mondfisch** (Mola mola): A=2 (rare pelagic), B=5 (heaviest bony fish + bizarre), C=2 (occasional Med), D=2 (unusual but hard to frame)
- **Blauhai** (Prionace glauca): A=2 (rare pelagic/open water), B=4 (high-seas wanderer + 50+ km daily), C=3 (summer Mediterranean pelagic), D=2 (blue coloration but fast)
- **Rochen** (Dasyatis pastinaca): A=4 (40-60% sandy bottom dives), B=3 (venomous spine + burial), C=4 (Med + North Atlantic), D=3 (buried in sand, hidden treasure)

---

### Tier 3: DACH Specialists (A=3-5, B=3-4, C=5, D=3-4)

Freshwater and cool-water species central to DACH lake/river diving. High encounter likelihood in home regions.

- **Flusskrebs** (Astacus astacus): A=4 (common limestone streams), B=3 (critically endangered + heritage), C=5 (DACH flagship), D=3 (claws + texture)
- **Hecht** (Esox lucius): A=5 (70%+ lake dives in weed zones), B=4 (ambush hunter + speed record), C=5 (DACH apex predator), D=4 (sleek + stalking)
- **Wels/Waller** (Silurus glanis): A=4 (50%+ deep hole dives), B=5 (massive catfish + barbels + size), C=5 (DACH big river legend), D=3 (murky freshwater, night dive specialty)
- **Süßwasserqualle** (Craspedacusta sowerbii): A=3 (summer warm-water events), B=3 (invasive + climate indicator), C=5 (emerging DACH lakes), D=1 (tiny + transparent but swarming)

---

### Tier 4: Bucket-List/Rare (A=1-2, B=3-5, C=2-4, D=3-5)

Rare, aspirational encounters; high engagement but low encounter likelihood.

- **Nacktschnecke/Nudibranch** (Chromodoris sp.): A=3 (10-30% rocky Med dives), B=4 (jewel colours + toxins), C=4 (Mediterranean), D=5 (macro masterpiece)
- **Seestern** (Asterias rubens): A=3 (15-40% Atlantic + Baltic rocky), B=2 (stomach eversion), C=3 (northern diver), D=3 (spiny texture)
- **Schildkröte** (Caretta caretta): A=1 (rare encounter), B=4 (magnetic navigation + site fidelity), C=3 (Mediterranean + Atlantic migrant), D=4 (graceful + documented)
- **Delfin** (Tursiops truncatus): A=1 (rare encounter), B=5 (high intelligence + echolocation + society), C=4 (Mediterranean year-round), D=2 (fast pelagic but gold when captured)

## Recommended Smoketest Strategy

**Priority sequence for image/content collection:**
1. **Immediate (Week 1):** Tier 1 species (Oktopus, Muräne, Zackenbarsch, Seepferdchen) — core app viability
2. **Week 2:** Tier 2 dramatic (Rotfeuerfisch, Rochen, Mondfisch) — engagement spike
3. **Week 3:** DACH Tier 3 (Hecht, Wels, Flusskreks) — market differentiation vs. Mediterranean-only competitors
4. **Week 4:** Tier 4 bucket-list (Schildkröte, Delfin, Nudibranchs) — aspirational content

**Validation gates:**
- A: Can we source 3+ quality images per species?
- B: Does the fun fact resonate in user interviews (test with diving forum posts)?
- C: Do divers in target regions recognize the species?
- D: Does the photo collection attract pin/share behaviour on Instagram/Pinterest?

## Regional Emphasis Notes

**Mediterranean Focus (60% of content):**
- Balearics, Croatia, Malta, Greece, southern France
- Year-round Tier 1 species (Oktopus, Muräne, Zackenbarsch, Seepferdchen)
- Seasonal Tier 2 (Rotfeuerfisch, Blauhai summer pelagic, Rochen)
- Tier 4 aspirational (Schildkröte, Delfin, Nudibranchs)

**DACH Freshwater Focus (30% of content):**
- Bodensee, Zürichsee, Chiemsee, Bavarian lakes, Alpine limestone streams
- Year-round Tier 3 (Hecht, Wels, Flusskrebs in specialized habitats)
- Emerging Tier 3 (Süßwasserqualle summer blooms)
- Domestic market differentiation vs. Mediterranean-only competitors

**Atlantic/Northern Bonus (10% of content):**
- Portugal, North Sea, Baltic pelagic encounters
- Seestern (Atlantic), Blauhai (vagrant), seasonal pelagic Mondfisch

---

## Content Adaptation Notes

- **Tier 1-2:** Feature full species profile, fun facts, safety notes, seasonal migration maps, conservation status
- **Tier 3 (Freshwater):** Emphasize "hidden treasures" angle — these species surprise freshwater divers who expect "boring lake diving"
- **Tier 4 (Bucket-list):** Aspirational pins, expedition guides, encounter stories from Diveboard/TripAdvisor diving reviews
- **Invasive (Rotfeuerfisch):** Frame as "emerging encounter" and "ecosystem indicator" — creates news hook and engagement
