## Part 1: Where to Post (Channel Strategy)

### 1.1 Pinterest (Primary — Visual Discovery)

**Why:** Mushroom foraging is peak Pinterest aesthetic. "Pilze sammeln" and "Pilzbestimmung" are high-volume German search terms. Foragers save guides and identification charts.

**Account:** Business account as "Pilzfang — Pilzkarten"

**Boards:**
- **Speisepilze Deutschlands** — Edible species cards with recipes
- **Giftige Pilze — Finger weg!** — Poison species cards (safety-critical, high engagement)
- **Pilz des Monats** — Monthly featured species
- **Pilzrezepte — Wald bis Teller** — Recipe-focused cards

**Posting cadence:** 3–5 pins/week during season (June–November), 1/week off-season.

**Feed:** Species cards in 1080×1350 feed format.

## Part 6: Image Sourcing Strategy

### Free Species Photos
- **iNaturalist API** — CC-licensed mushroom observations. Filter by `iconic_taxon_name=Fungi`, Germany. Programmatic access via `pyinaturalist`.
- **Wikimedia Commons** — Public domain + CC mushroom photos. Extensive coverage of European species.
- **Pixabay** — 10,000+ mushroom images, CC0 license.

### Photo Outreach (Personal Photos)
- **Pilzforum.eu** — Ask members for permission to feature their finds
- **Local Pilzvereine** — Bayern mushroom clubs, annual forays
- **Own forays** — Document finds during season (June–November)

### AI-Generated Imagery
- Use for **branded backgrounds and textures only** — NOT for species identification images
- AI-generated mushroom images risk anatomical inaccuracies that could mislead foragers
- Nano Banana Pro is suitable for: banners, app icons, atmospheric backgrounds, marketing materials

### Licensing Notes
- iNaturalist: CC-BY or CC-BY-NC depending on photographer's choice — check per image
- Wikimedia: Mixed licenses — verify each image's license page
- Pixabay: CC0 (no attribution required, commercial use OK)
- Personal photos: Permission logged in `docs/smoketest-tracking.csv`
